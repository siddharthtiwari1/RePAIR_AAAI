
\section{Conclusion}
We introduced Interactive Machine Unlearning (IMU), a new problem setting in which end users instruct an LLM to forget targeted knowledge through natural language at inference time, removing the dependency on model service providers. To realize IMU, we proposed RePAIR, a multi-model framework in which $\mathcal{M}_{\textit{watchdog}}$ detects unlearning intent from the conversation history, $\mathcal{M}_{\textit{surgeon}}$ generates executable repair code, and $\mathcal{M}_{\textit{patient}}$ undergoes autonomous weight modification. At its core lies STAMP, a training-free, single-sample unlearning method that redirects MLP activations toward a refusal subspace through a closed-form pseudoinverse update, together with its low-rank variant STAMP-LR. We validated RePAIR across three unlearning tasks, namely harmful knowledge suppression, misinformation correction, and personal data erasure, where it achieves near-zero forget scores while preserving model utility and delivers up to a $\sim$3$\times$ speedup over SoTA methods.

\noindent
\textbf{Future work.} Removing the dependence on a retain buffer, so that the update is driven by $\mathcal{D}_f$ alone in the manner of FLAT~\cite{wang2024llm}, would close the remaining gap between RePAIR and the privacy motivation on which it rests. Extending STAMP to multimodal foundation models is a natural next step, since the same linear down-projection appears in the feed-forward block of every modality encoder and the update rule transfers unchanged.
