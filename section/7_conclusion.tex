
\section{Conclusion}
We introduced Interactive Machine Unlearning (IMU), an inference-time setting in which users instruct an LLM to forget through natural language, under two constraints no prior method satisfies, namely training-free and single-sample operation. RePAIR realizes it with $\mathcal{M}_{\textit{watchdog}}$ detecting intent, $\mathcal{M}_{\textit{surgeon}}$ writing the repair, and STAMP editing $\mathcal{M}_{\textit{patient}}$ by redirecting MLP activations toward a refusal subspace through a closed-form pseudoinverse update, alongside its low-rank variant STAMP-LR. Across harmful knowledge suppression, misinformation correction, and personal data erasure it reaches near-zero forget scores while preserving utility, at up to $\sim$3$\times$ the speed of SoTA methods. Removing the retain buffer, in the manner of FLAT~\cite{wang2024llm}, and extending STAMP to multimodal encoders, which expose the same down-projection, are natural next steps.
